# Setting Up Your Vault

## 1. Before you start

Make sure cowork-ai-os is installed and onboarded. Watch Nuno's install video first. The video shows you which buttons to press.

## 2. The three vault modes

**Already have a vault.** The wizard finds it. It points Claude at it. You keep all your old notes.

**Want a fresh start.** The wizard makes a new vault for you. It builds the folders. It is ready to use right away.

**Have an old vault you want Claude in.** This is overlay mode. The wizard adds the new structure on top. It does not touch your old notes.

## 3. Where the vault lives (the cloud-sync rule)

> **Your vault must live on your local drive only. Never in iCloud, OneDrive, Dropbox, or Google Drive.**

### Why

Cloud sync corrupts Obsidian vaults. Two computers can save at the same time. Files get duplicated. Notes go missing.

This happens 95% of the time when sync is on. The wizard will refuse to set up your vault in a cloud folder.

### Safe paths per OS

| OS | Safe path |
|---|---|
| Mac | `~/SecondBrain/` |
| Windows | `C:\Users\<your-name>\SecondBrain\` |
| Linux | `~/SecondBrain/` |

### Bad paths to avoid

- Anything inside `OneDrive` or `OneDrive - <Org>` (Windows + Mac)
- Anything inside `iCloud Drive` or `Library/Mobile Documents/` (Mac)
- Anything inside `Dropbox`
- Anything inside `Google Drive` or `Library/CloudStorage/GoogleDrive-` (Mac)
- Windows 11 default `Documents/` folder (it is OneDrive-synced unless you turned that off)

### If your old vault is in a cloud folder

The wizard will offer to move it for you. Say yes. It will close Obsidian. Then it copies the vault to a safe folder and points Obsidian at the new spot.

### Mac case-sensitivity gotcha

On Mac, `Raw/` and `raw/` are different folders. The wizard uses lowercase. Don't change it.

## 4. What the wizard creates

```
SecondBrain/
├── about-me/         (who you are. Claude reads this.)
├── work/             (one of your life areas. You can have up to 4.)
│   ├── raw/          (you write here)
│   ├── wiki/         (Claude writes here. Don't touch.)
│   └── output/       (you and Claude work here together)
├── wiki/
│   └── hot.md        (the short cache Claude reads first)
└── _wiki-prompts.md  (the recipes for build, update, health-check)
```

Each life area has the same three folders: raw, wiki, output. You can have up to four life areas. Don't add more than that. Claude gets confused.

## 5. How to run the wizard

1. Type `/onboard-second-brain` in your Cowork chat.
2. Answer the questions. The wizard saves your spot so you can stop and pick it up later.
3. Watch Nuno's install video while it runs. The video shows the press-here moments.

The wizard takes about 10 minutes. Don't rush.

## 6. About the about-me folder

The wizard makes 3 small files in `about-me/`: who you are, how you write, and how you want Claude to talk to you. Claude reads these every time you start a vault session. Keep them short.

## 7. Optional: Obsidian Sync

If you want your vault on your phone or another computer, Obsidian has a paid sync option. It costs $5 a month. It is end-to-end encrypted, which means even Obsidian can't read your notes.

**The encryption key is unrecoverable.** If you lose it, your notes are gone. Save the key in a password manager.

## 8. If something goes wrong

Most install problems show up at the cloud-sync check or the MCP test. Lesson 03 has a list of common problems and quick fixes. If you get stuck, describe what you see to Claude and ask it to help.
