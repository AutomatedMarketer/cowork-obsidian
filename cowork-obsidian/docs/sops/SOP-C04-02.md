# How to Use It Day-to-Day

## 1. The daily loop

You write. Claude organizes. You ask. Claude answers from your notes.

That's the whole loop. Everything below is how to make each step a habit.

## 2. What goes in `raw/`

Drop these into `raw/` as you go:

- Voice memos you turned into text — paste the text. Claude cleans it up later.
- Screenshots with one sentence about why you saved them.
- Brain dumps after a meeting — just type what's in your head.
- Articles you want to remember — paste the link plus your one-sentence takeaway.
- Journal entries — anything from your day.
- Half-formed ideas you don't want to lose.

The rule is simple: if you might want to find it again, drop it in raw.

## 3. What goes in `wiki/`

**Nothing. You never write here. Claude does it for you.**

If you write in wiki by hand, you will break the system. Want to fix something Claude wrote? Add a note in raw that says what's wrong. The next UPDATE will fix it.

## 4. What goes in `output/`

Things you make for the world. A blog post. A client doc. A pitch deck.

You and Claude work on these together. Save the final version here so Claude can fold the lessons back into the wiki later.

## 5. The Sunday ritual (UPDATE)

This is the one you must not skip. Here is how it goes:

- Pick one time a week. Sunday at 5pm with a coffee works for most people.
- Open Claude Cowork.
- Type `/second-brain update`.
- Claude reads everything new in `raw/` and grows the wiki.
- Read what Claude changed. If something looks off, tell Claude. It will fix it.
- Done in about 10 minutes.

UPDATE is safe. It only adds and improves. It never deletes. (BUILD is the destructive one — you almost never run it. The wizard handled BUILD for you on day one.)

**This is the one habit that makes everything else work.** Skip a week and your wiki gets stale. Skip a month and you stop using it.

**Rhythm beats enthusiasm. Doing UPDATE for 10 years beats doing it perfectly for 3 weeks.**

## 6. The monthly ritual

### HEALTH-CHECK

First Saturday of each month. Run `/second-brain health-check`. Claude reads the whole wiki and tells you what's broken, missing, or saying two different things at once. Fix what matters. Ignore what doesn't.

### REFRESH-HOT

Same day. Run `/second-brain refresh-hot`. Claude rewrites the small `wiki/hot.md` cache so it knows what's current. Takes 1 minute.

## 7. The quarterly ritual (FOLD-BACK)

First Sunday of January, April, July, and October. Run `/second-brain fold-back`. Claude takes everything in `output/` and folds the lessons back into the wiki. The wiki gets sharper every quarter.

## 8. How to ask the vault questions

Type `/open-vault`. Then ask Claude anything.

Example: "What do I know about pricing?" Claude looks at the index, picks the wiki pages that match, then answers. You don't load every note. You only load what you need.

## 9. The three habits that kill a second brain

- **Don't edit the wiki by hand.** Put corrections in raw. Let UPDATE handle it.
- **Don't dump your old ChatGPT history all at once.** Pull useful pieces in slowly, as you need them.
- **Don't expect it to work without raw input.** Garbage in equals nothing out. Write something in raw every day.

## 10. The librarian metaphor

Think of Claude as a librarian. You drop notes on the desk in `raw/`. The librarian reads them, sorts them, and writes clean books in `wiki/`. When you ask a question, the librarian walks straight to the right book. That's all this is.

## 11. Calendar setup

Add three events to your calendar so you don't have to remember:

1. Weekly: Sunday 5pm — UPDATE
2. Monthly: First Saturday — HEALTH-CHECK + REFRESH-HOT
3. Quarterly: First Sunday of Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct — FOLD-BACK

The wizard offers to make these for you. Say yes.
