# Why a Second Brain

## 1. What is a second brain?

A second brain is a place to keep what you learn. It is a folder of notes on your computer. Claude can read every note in it. That means Claude remembers what you know.

Think of it like a giant notebook you never lose. You write things down. Claude reads them later. You do not have to retype old stuff every time you ask for help.

## 2. Why does Claude need one?

Right now Claude forgets things between chats. You can have a great talk on Monday. By Friday Claude does not remember any of it. The vault is the fix. It gives Claude a place to look up what you have said before.

## 3. The big idea (Karpathy)

Andrej Karpathy is one of the people who built ChatGPT. In April he wrote: *"Obsidian is the IDE, the LLM is the programmer, the wiki is the codebase. You rarely ever write or edit the wiki manually."*

That is a fancy way of saying two things. Obsidian holds your notes. Claude does the writing. You do not type up the wiki yourself. Claude does that part for you.

Source: https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f

## 4. How Claude uses your notes (the four memory tiers)

Claude does not read every note every time. That would be too slow. It reads in four steps.

| Tier | What | When Claude reads it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Who you are (your `about-me/`) | Whenever a skill needs it |
| 2 | A short cache (`wiki/hot.md`) | At the start of every vault session |
| 3 | A list of all your notes (`wiki/_index.md`) | When you ask a question that needs lookup |
| 4 | The full notes themselves | Only when one of them is the right answer |

Claude only loads what it needs when it needs it. That way your notes do not slow down every chat.

## 5. How the vault works

Julie Chenell came up with this way of thinking about it: *"There's a human in raw, a robot in wiki, and a human AND a robot in output."*

Your vault has three folders inside each life area. Each one has a job.

- `raw/` — you write here. Brain dumps. Voice notes. Screenshots. Half-baked ideas. Anything goes.
- `wiki/` — Claude writes here. Clean notes built from your raw stuff. Never edit this by hand.
- `output/` — you and Claude work here together. Things you make for the world go here.

## 6. The four rules

Four rules keep the vault simple.

- One vault.
- Up to four life areas. Not more.
- Each life area has exactly three folders: `raw`, `wiki`, `output`.
- One `about-me/` folder at the top.

That is the whole shape. Same in every life area. Easy to remember.

## 7. The promise

By the end of these four lessons, Claude will know about every note you have ever written.

## 8. The hardest part

This works only if you actually use it. A second brain you never write in is just an empty folder. The next lesson sets you up. The lesson after that teaches you how to use it every day.
